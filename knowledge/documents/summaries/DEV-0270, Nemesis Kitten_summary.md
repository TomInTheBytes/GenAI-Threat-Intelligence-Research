
Report 1

Summary:
The threat actor DEV-0270, also known as Nemesis Kitten, is a subgroup of Iranian actor PHOSPHORUS. They have been involved in ransomware campaigns targeting victims in various sectors, with a focus on financial gain. Microsoft attributes malicious network operations, including vulnerability scanning, to DEV-0270 on behalf of the Iranian government. However, some attacks seem to be for personal or company-specific revenue generation. The threat actor has been observed using tools like Impacket, WmiExec, and Living off the Land. The activity of DEV-0270 was first seen in 2022, with the latest source modification in 2023. Their operations show a capability for sophisticated cyber attacks, indicating a high level of expertise.





Report 2

Summary:
The threat actor DEV-0270, also known as Nemesis Kitten, is a sub-group of the Iranian actor PHOSPHORUS. Microsoft assesses that DEV-0270 conducts malicious network operations on behalf of the government of Iran, but some ransomware attacks may be for personal or company-specific revenue generation. DEV-0270 targets organizations globally, leveraging exploits for high-severity vulnerabilities and using living-off-the-land binaries (LOLBINs) for discovery and credential access. The group demands USD 8,000 for decryption keys and has been observed pursuing other income-generating avenues, such as selling stolen data. DEV-0270 is linked to companies operating under the aliases Secnerd and Lifeweb in Iran. The threat actor uses various tactics, including disabling security features, creating new user accounts, and using PowerShell for malicious activities. Microsoft provides mitigation steps and detection details to help organizations defend against DEV-0270's attacks. The group's activities have been observed in various campaigns, targeting vulnerable servers and devices globally.
